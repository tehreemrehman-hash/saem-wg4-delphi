Welcome to Workgroup 4

Thank you for joining the 2026 SAEM Consensus Conference effort to define a 10-year research agenda for AI in emergency medicine. Workgroup 4 is focused on the most distinctly human dimensions of this work: how AI affects clinical reasoning, professional identity, burnout, trust, and the patient experience.

This kickoff meeting orients the group to the modified Delphi process, the 18 candidate research questions, and the timeline leading up to our in-person consensus session on May 21, 2026 in Atlanta.

WG4 Scope & Domains

Our 18 candidate questions are organized across four domains:

  • Domain A — Clinician–AI Interaction & Clinical Reasoning (Q1–Q6): automation bias, explainability, trust calibration, alert burden, human factors
  • Domain B — Professional Identity, Wellbeing & Autonomy (Q7–Q11): identity, burnout, agency, clinician–patient relationship, oversight
  • Domain C — Patient Experience & Shared Decision-Making (Q12–Q15): patient perception, SDM tools, anthropomorphism, disclosure
  • Domain D — System Design & Implementation (Q16–Q18): workflow integration, adaptive systems, outcome measures

The Modified Delphi Process

  1. Round 1 (this meeting → mid-point): Each member rates all 18 questions Include / Include with Modifications / Exclude, with optional comments.
  2. Mid-point review: We share aggregated Round 1 results, discuss disagreements, and refine wording.
  3. Round 2: Members re-rate the (possibly revised) set in light of group feedback.
  4. May 21, Atlanta: Final breakouts, cross-group discussion, and full-audience consensus vote.

Consensus threshold: ≥80% Include for a question to be confirmed.
